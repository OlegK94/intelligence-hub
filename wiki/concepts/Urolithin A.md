# Urolithin A

## Overview

**Urolithin A** is a metabolite produced by gut bacteria from polyphenols found in pomegranates, berries, and nuts (specifically ellagitannins). It is notable for its ability to induce **mitophagy** — the selective autophagy (clearance) of damaged mitochondria — making it one of the most researched longevity compounds.

## Mechanisms of Action

| Mechanism | Effect |
|---|---|
| **Mitophagy induction** | Selectively removes damaged mitochondria, improving mitochondrial quality |
| **Mitochondrial biogenesis** | Stimulates growth of new, healthy mitochondria |
| **Muscle function** | Improves muscle strength and endurance (studied in aging populations) |
| **Anti-inflammatory** | Reduces inflammatory markers |
| **NAD+ pathway** | Some interaction with NAD+ metabolism |

## Clinical Evidence

- Studied at 500mg–1000mg doses by [[Amazentis]] (MITOPURE)
- Clinical trials: improved muscle endurance in older adults
- Used in Bryan Johnson's Blueprint protocol
- Considered a premium longevity ingredient alongside [[NMN]]

## Regulatory Status — CRITICAL BLOCKER

### EU Novel Food Authorization

[[Amazentis]] obtained **EU Novel Food authorization** for Urolithin A as **MITOPURE**:
- Authorization is **proprietary** to Amazentis during the protection period (typically 5 years)
- After proprietary period expires → authorization becomes generic
- **Current status of proprietary period:** Must be researched (is it expired or active?)

### Patent Landscape

[[Amazentis]] holds patents covering:
- Use of Urolithin A as a supplement/food ingredient
- Specific synthesis/extraction processes
- Health claim applications

**Dual barrier:** Both patent licensing AND Novel Food compliance required.
