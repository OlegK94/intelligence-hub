# ALG I Progressionsvorbehalt

## Overview

The **Progressionsvorbehalt** (§ 32b EStG) is a German income tax mechanism by which certain **steuerfreie Einnahmen** (tax-free income) — such as [[ALG I 2025]] — are excluded from direct taxation but **raise the applicable tax rate** on all remaining taxable income.

## How It Works

### Step 1: Add ALG I to taxable income (fictionally)
For the purpose of rate calculation, ALG I is added to the sum of taxable income.

### Step 2: Calculate the applicable tax rate
The effective tax rate is determined on this **higher fictitious income**.

### Step 3: Apply the higher rate to actual taxable income only
The elevated tax rate is applied only to the **actual taxable income** (GF salary, employment income). The ALG I itself is not taxed.

### Result
The taxpayer pays **more tax on their employment income** than they would have without the ALG I — even though the ALG I is tax-free.

## Statutory Basis

- **§32b EStG** — Progressionsvorbehalt mechanism
- **§46 EStG** — Pflichtveranlagung triggered when Lohnersatzleistungen > 410 €/Jahr
  - With ~8.400 € ALG I received, §46 EStG threshold is clearly exceeded
  - Filing the [[ESt 2025]] Steuererklärung is therefore **mandatory** (not optional)

## Magnitude for Oleg (2025)

With the now-known figure of **~8.400 € ALG I** (until ~June 2025):

| Scenario | Taxable Income | ALG I | Steuersatz Basis | Tax Paid |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Without ALG I | ~GF salary | 0 € | lower bracket | lower |
| With ALG I | ~GF salary | +8.400 € | higher bracket | significantly more |

> ⚠️ **Note:** The exact magnitude depends on the total Wagglz GF salary for 2025 (see [[Wagglz GF-Gehalt 2025]]) and any other taxable income. The ~8.400 € ALG I is a meaningful addition that will measurably increase the effective rate on the GF salary.

## Tax Filing Implication

- Must be declared in **Anlage N** of the Einkommensteuererklärung
- **ALG-I-Bescheinigung** (Jahresbescheinigung) from Bundesagentur für Arbeit is required — must be obtained for [[ESt 2025]]
- [[SP STB]] (Steuerberater) must ensure correct calculation
- Incorrect handling is a common filing error — understating or omitting ALG I
- §46 EStG makes filing **mandatory** (Pflichtveranlagung)
