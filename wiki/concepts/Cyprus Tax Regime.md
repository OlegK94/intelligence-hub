# Cyprus Tax Regime

## Overview

Cyprus offers one of the most favorable personal tax regimes in the EU for high-earning new residents, particularly employees. This concept page documents the key provisions relevant to [[Oleg Personal Context|Oleg]]'s [[Cyprus Relocation]] plan.

## Key Provisions

### 50% Income Tax Exemption (Neuankömmlinge)

- **Scope:** 50% of employment income is exempt from personal income tax
- **Eligibility:** New residents who were not Cyprus tax residents in the prior 10 years
- **Minimum salary:** Approximately **55,000 €/year** (threshold must be met)
- **Duration:** Up to **17 years** from first employment in Cyprus
- **Applicable to:** Employment income, including B2B SaaS Sales roles — unlike [[Portugal IFICI]] which explicitly excludes sales professions

### Physical Presence Requirement

> ⚠️ **Assumption [A]:** The 50% exemption likely requires at least **60 days of physical presence** in Cyprus per year. This must be confirmed by a [[Deutsch-Zypriotischer Steuerberater]].

Cyprus tax residency is established by either:
1. **183-day rule** — spending more than 183 days per year in Cyprus, OR
2. **60-day rule** — spending at least 60 days in Cyprus (if not a tax resident elsewhere for >183 days)

### Non-Domicile Status

- Cyprus offers **Non-Domicile** status for new residents
- Key benefit: **No tax on dividends or interest income** (relevant for GmbH distributions — see [[Oleg Business Entity Structure]])
- Duration: Available for 17 years for qualifying individuals

### Corporate Tax Rate

- Cyprus corporate tax rate: **12.5%** — one of the lowest in the EU
- Relevant for any Cyprus-based entity structure

## Double Taxation Treaty (DE-CY)

Germany and Cyprus have a **Doppelbesteuerungsabkommen** (double taxation treaty). Key implications:
- Prevents the same income from being taxed in both countries
- Relevant for [[DoktorLib]] salary income during transition period
- Must be analyzed by a [[Deutsch-Zypriotischer Steuerberater]] in context of:
  - **Exit-Besteuerung** (Wegzugsteuer) — German exit tax on unrealized gains
  - **GmbH permanent establishment** risk if GmbH remains active in Germany

## EOR Interaction

For the 50% exemption to apply:
- Oleg must be **employed** (not just a contractor) in Cyprus
- This requires either a Cyprus-registered employer or an **Employer of Record (EOR)** arrangement
- EOR providers like **Deel** or **Remote.com** can establish this structure
- Cost of EOR is a gate item — see [[Cyprus Relocation Detail]]

## Risk Factors

1. **Physical presence constraint** — 60-day minimum may conflict with [[Café Berlin Partnership Hai]] Berlin presence requirements
2. **Partner EOR** — partner's employer (cosmetics/PM) may not support remote Cyprus arrangement
3. **GmbH complexity** — active German GmbH + Cyprus residency requires careful structuring
4. **Wegzugsteuer** — exit tax on GmbH shares upon leaving Germany
