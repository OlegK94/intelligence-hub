# BMR and TDEE

## Definitions

### BMR — Basal Metabolic Rate
The number of calories the body burns at complete rest to maintain basic physiological functions (breathing, circulation, cell production). Measured via the 3D body scan metabolic assessment.

### TDEE — Total Daily Energy Expenditure
Total calories burned per day, accounting for all activity levels on top of BMR. Calculated as:
```
TDEE = BMR × Activity Multiplier
```

## Implied Activity Multiplier

TDEE / BMR = 3,645 / 2,113 ≈ **1.72**

This is consistent with a **very active** activity multiplier (typical range: 1.725 for very active individuals with 6-day training splits). Consistent with [[Health Protocol Master]]'s 6-day training protocol.

## Caloric Strategy

- **Target deficit:** ~845 kcal/day from TDEE
- **Goal:** Fat loss from 20.3% → 15–17% body fat
- **Protein target (lean-mass-based):** 195–200g/day (~2.6g/kg lean mass × 77.1 kg)
- **Protein target (body-weight-based / operational):** ~220g/day (~2.3g/kg × 96.7 kg) — per [[Claude Session Template Performance]]
- **Strategy:** Significant deficit with high protein to minimize muscle loss during cut

> ⚠️ **Protein target discrepancy:** The scan-document-derived figure is 195–200 g/day; the Claude session template uses ~220 g/day as the operational target. Both calculations are valid using different reference bases. The 220 g/day figure is the one actively used in day-to-day AI-assisted tracking.

## Relationship to Lean Mass Targets

At 2,800 kcal with ~220g protein (~880 kcal from protein), the remaining ~1,920 kcal comes from fat and carbohydrates. This structure is designed to:
1. Preserve 77.1 kg lean mass (FFMI 21.3)
2. Reduce fat mass from 19.6 kg → ~14 kg
3. Support 6-day training load
