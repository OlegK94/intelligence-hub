# Longevity Wirkstoffe — Novel Food Status (Phase 3+)

## Overview

The **Phase 3+ Longevity ingredient stack** (planned for 2027+) is **blocked** by regulatory and licensing complexity. Three key wirkstoffe have distinct status profiles that must be resolved before Phase 3 launch.

---

## Ingredient Status Matrix

### 1. **NMN (Nicotinamide Mononucleotide)**

| Aspect | Status |
|---|---|
| **Patent Holder** | [[Chromadex]] (USA + EU portfolio) |
| **Patent Expiry** | Post-2030 (estimated; needs verification) |
| **EFSA Opinion** | ✅ Positive (May 2026, EffePharm) |
| **EU Authorization** | ⏳ Pending (EC authorization awaited) |
| **FTO Status** | Blockiert; license required from Chromadex OR wait for patent expiry |
| **Licensing Cost** | Unknown; likely €50k–500k+ (proprietary negotiation) |
| **Launch Blocker** | EC authorization + Chromadex licensing |

**Implication:** Even with positive EFSA opinion, EC has not yet authorized NMN for use in EU foods/supplements. Chromadex likely waiting for EC authorization before licensing to third parties. Timeline: Could be 6–24 months.

### 2. **Urolithin A**

| Aspect | Status |
|---|---|
| **Patent Holder / Licensor** | [[Amazentis]] (proprietär Novel Food license) |
| **License Exclusive** | Yes; until ~2034 (patent protection) |
| **EFSA Opinion** | ✅ Pre-approved (2022, novel food authorization) |
| **EU Authorization** | ✅ Authorized (under Amazentis license terms) |
| **FTO Status** | Blockiert; license from Amazentis required (OR use similar ingredient post-2034) |
| **Licensing Cost** | Unknown; likely €100k–1M+ (proprietary negotiation, milestone-based) |
| **Launch Blocker** | Amazentis licensing negotiation; cost unknown |

**Implication:** Amazentis holds monopoly until 2034. Any brand wishing to use Urolithin A must:
- Negotiate proprietary license (expensive, uncertain timeline)
- OR wait until patent expires (~2034) and formulate generic Urolithin A
- OR use alternative senolytic/longevity compounds (not Urolithin A specific)

### 3. **Spermidin**

| Aspect | Status |
|---|---|
| **Allowed Form** | Wheat germ extract ONLY |
| **Max Daily Dose** | ≤6 mg/day (NEM segment; not pharmaceutical) |
| **Patent Status** | Multiple wheat-germ manufacturers; FTO complex |
| **Format Limitation** | **NOT permitted in beverage formats** (only NEM capsules/powder) |
| **EFSA Opinion** | ✅ Approved for NEM (but NOT for functional food drinks) |
| **FTO Status** | Green for NEM; Red for beverage formats |
| **Launch Blocker** | Format constraint; cannot be added to coffee beverage (Phase 1 SKU) |

**Implication:** Spermidin is only viable as a **separate NEM capsule product** (Phase 3b+), not integrated into the coffee beverage stack. Practical impact: two SKUs required (coffee + capsule combo) or capsule-only Phase 3b entry.

## Blocking Status for Phase 3 Launch

### Launch Timeline Dependency

| Ingredient | Status | Resolution Path | Timeline |
|---|---|---|---|
| **NMN** | 🔴 Blockiert | EC authorization + Chromadex licensing | 6–24 months |
| **Urolithin A** | 🔴 Blockiert | Amazentis licensing negotiation (cost unknown) | 3–12 months OR wait 8 years |
| **Spermidin** | 🟡 Partial | Separate NEM product + wheat-germ sourcing | 3–6 months (if pursued) |

**Summary:** Phase 3 launch is realistically delayed until **2027–2028** pending:
1. EC authorization of NMN (EC decision, not in Aevum's control)
2. Amazentis licensing negotiation (cost + terms unknown)
3. Either/both of the above resolved satisfactorily
