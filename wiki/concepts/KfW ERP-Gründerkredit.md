# KfW ERP-Gründerkredit

## Overview

The **KfW ERP-Gründerkredit** is a German state-backed startup and small business loan program offered through **KfW** (Kreditanstalt für Wiederaufbau), Germany's federal development bank. It is listed as a financing instrument to be investigated during the [[Café Berlin Partnership Hai]] capital structure planning session.

## What It Is

- **Type:** Low-interest startup/SME loan
- **Issuer:** KfW (federal development bank), backed by ERP (European Recovery Program) funds
- **Target:** New businesses, young companies (typically < 5 years old), self-employed
- **Key benefit:** Below-market interest rates and favorable repayment terms

## Relevance to Café Berlin

In the [[Café Berlin Partnership Hai — Planning Detail]] document, the KfW ERP-Gründerkredit is listed under **Kapitalstruktur** (capital structure) as a debt financing option to:
- Reduce the equity each partner needs to contribute at founding
- Cover startup costs: Umbau (renovation) + Erstausstattung (initial equipment) + Mietpuffer (rent buffer)

## Key Loan Parameters (General)

| Parameter | Typical Range |
|---|---|
| Loan amount | Up to €125,000 (ERP-Gründerkredit Startgeld) or higher for other programs |
| Interest rate | Variable, typically 1–5% p.a. (subsidized) |
| Term | 5–10 years |
| Grace period | Up to 1–2 years (no repayment of principal) |
| Collateral | Often required; personal liability |

## Application Process

- Applied through a **Hausbank** (commercial bank) — KfW does not lend directly to borrowers
- Requires business plan (Businessplan), financial projections, and personal creditworthiness check
- EU citizenship / German residency typically required

## Consideration for Partnership Structure

When applied to a GmbH or GbR:
- Both partners may need to provide personal guarantees
- Loan goes to the legal entity, not individuals
