## A) 12 Hallmarks of Aging — Addressability Summary

### Framework: López-Otín 2023

*Source: López-Otín C. et al., "Hallmarks of aging: An expanding universe", Cell 186(2), 2023*

The 2023 update (from 9 to 12 hallmarks) organized them into three tiers:

| Tier | Hallmarks |
|---|---|
| **Primary** (causes) | Genomic instability, Telomere attrition, Epigenetic alterations, Proteostasis loss |
| **Antagonistic** (protective then harmful) | Deregulated nutrient sensing, Mitochondrial dysfunction, Cellular senescence |
| **Integrative** (result from above) | Stem cell exhaustion, Altered intercellular communication, Inflammaging, Dysbiosis, Mechanical properties (NEW 2023) |

### Hallmark Addressability Matrix

| Hallmark | Rating | Schlüsselsubstanzen |
|---|---|---|
| Mitochondriale Dysfunktion | ★★★★ | Chlorogensäure, CoQ10, Urolithin A, NMN |
| Inflammaging | ★★★★ | Chlorogensäure, Cafestol, Curcumin |
| AMPK/mTOR/Sirtuine | ★★★★★ | Koffein, Chlorogensäure, Trigonellin, NMN |
| Zelluläre Seneszenz | ★★★ | Quercetin, Fisetin (Pulsing-Protokoll) |
| Proteostase | ★★★ | Spermidine, Koffein, Lion's Mane |

| Telomer-Attrition | ★ | Kaum adressierbar durch Nahrung |
| Dysbiose | ★★★ | Kaffee → Bifidus ↑, Bacteroidetes ↑ |

## B) Kaffee als Longevity-Substanz

### Chlorogensäure (CGA)

**Chemistry:** Ester of caffeic acid + quinic acid; main polyphenol in green coffee. 1 cup filtered coffee = 70–350 mg CGA depending on roast.

**Key mechanisms:**
1. AMPK activation via CAMKK2 pathway; hepatocyte glucose uptake +15–30%
   *Source: Ong KW et al., BBRC 2012*
2. Insulin sensitivity: RCT (n=56, T2DM risk): 400 mg CGA/12 weeks → fasting glucose −8 mg/dL, HOMA-IR −0.4
   *Source: Johnston KL et al., AJCN 2003*
3. GLP-1 potentiation: CGA increases postprandial GLP-1 secretion
   *Source: Zheng G et al., J Agric Food Chem 2004*
4. NF-κB inhibition: direct anti-inflammatory in macrophages

**Roast gradient:**
- Light Roast (150–160°C): ~250–350 mg/cup
- Dark Roast (220°C+): ~50–100 mg/cup
- **Recommendation: Light/Medium Roast**

**Robusta vs. Arabica:** Vietnamese Robusta contains 7–12% CGA (dry weight) vs. Arabica 5–8% → Robusta advantage for CGA content.

### Trigonellin as NAD+ Precursor — 2024 Discovery

**Key finding (Nature Metabolism, March 2024):** Trigonellin (coffee alkaloid) → nicotinic acid (niacin/B3) in gut → NAD+ via Preiss-Handler pathway.

*Source: Kashyap S. et al., "Trigonelline is an NAD+ precursor that improves age-related muscle decline", Nature Metabolism 6, 2024*

**Content by roast:**
- Green Robusta: 0.6–1.2% dry weight
- Green Arabica: 0.9–1.4% dry weight
- **Heat sensitive: 50–80% lost in dark roast**
- Light/Medium Roast: ~30–50 mg trigonellin per cup (250 ml)

**Pathway:** Trigonellin → Nikotinsäure → NAMN → NAD+ (Preiss-Handler-Pathway)
- Slower than direct NMN/NR supplementation, but physiologically valid
- 2024 Human study: trigonellin correlates with NAD+ levels; supplementation increased NAD+ in muscle tissue

**Strategic claim:** "Light-Roast coffee contains trigonellin — a natural NAD+ precursor." No Novel Food problem (naturally occurring in food). Authentic, scientifically supported.

### Cafestol and Kahweol

**Identity:** Diterpenes found exclusively in coffee beans; extracted primarily when brewed without paper filter (French Press, Espresso, Mokka). Paper filter removes ~95%.

| Effect | Direction | Clinical Relevance |
|---|---|---|
| LDL-Cholesterol | +0.13 mmol/L per 10 mg cafestol/day | Concern for cardiovascular risk |
| Hepatoprotective | NRF2-Aktivierung, Phase-II-Enzyme ↑ | Animal data good; human limited |
| Anti-carcinogenic | Apoptosis induction in cancer cells (in vitro) | Preclinical only |
| Anti-inflammatory | COX-2 inhibition | In vitro |

**Product relevance:** Paper-filter-compatible instant base → spray-drying process removes diterpenes largely → **LDL-neutral** in instant format. Consistent with Bryan Johnson's filtered coffee preference.

*Sources: Urgert R. & Katan MB., NEJM 1997; Tverdal A. et al., European Journal of Preventive Cardiology 2020*

### Epidemiology: Coffee and Mortality

**Optimal dose:** 3–5 cups/day (J-curve); RR −12–15% vs. 0 cups for all-cause mortality.

*Source: Poole R. et al., "Coffee consumption and health: umbrella review", BMJ 2017, n=3.8M, 40 studies*

**Disease-specific RR reductions (3–5 cups/day vs. 0):**

| Disease | RR Reduction | Evidence Quality |
|---|---|---|
| Typ-2-Diabetes | −25–30% | Sehr gut (GRADE A) |
| Parkinson | −30–35% | Gut |
| Alzheimer | −25–30% | Gut |
| Lebererkrankungen (Zirrhose, Karzinom) | −40–50% | Sehr gut |
| KHK | −10–15% | Moderat |
| Darmkrebs | −15–20% | Gut |
| Gesamtmortalität | −12–15% | Sehr gut |

**Caffeinated vs. decaffeinated:** Many benefits persist with decaf → polyphenols (CGA, trigonellin) co-responsible. Caffeine adds thermogenesis and cognitive effects.

## C) Biomarker Profile

### Stack-Relevant Biomarkers

| Biomarker | What it Measures | Stack Effect | Expected Change |
|---|---|---|---|
| **hs-CRP** | Systemic inflammation | CGA, Curcumin, NAC | ↓ 15–30% |
| **HbA1c** | 3-month glucose average | CGA (AMPK↑), Koffein | ↓ 0.1–0.3% in pre-diabetics |
| **Nüchtern-Glucose / HOMA-IR** | Insulin sensitivity | CGA directly | ↓ 5–10 mg/dL |
| **ApoB** | Atherogene LDL particle count | Neutral with filtered/instant | Neutral |
| **NAD+ (blood/muscle)** | Cellular energetics; sirtuin activity | Trigonellin → NAD+ | ↑ measurable with supplementation |
| **IGF-1** | GH axis; longevity context | Koffein little impact | Largely unaffected |
| **Ferritin / Hämatologie** | Iron status | Coffee inhibits Fe absorption 30–60% | Warning: timing important |
| **Telomere length (optional)** | Biological age | Antioxidants moderately positive | No short-term change |

**Key warning:** Coffee inhibits iron absorption by 30–60% → important for timing (not on empty stomach with iron deficiency).

## Critical Limitations

1. Causal human RCTs for longevity outcomes are nearly nonexistent (ethics: decades-long studies impossible)
2. Many mechanism data from in vitro / animal models → human transferability probable but unproven
3. EFSA does not permit "Anti-Aging" claims → must communicate via biomarker language
4. Dose-response relationships for individual components in complex mixtures unknown
