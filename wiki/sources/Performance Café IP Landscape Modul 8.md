# Performance Café — IP & Patent-Landschaft (Modul 8)

## Overview

This source document (`raw/Business/PerformanceCafe/legal/ip_landscape.md`, status: in-arbeit, date: 2026-06-14) is the comprehensive IP and regulatory research document for [[Performance Coffee Brand]]. ## 1. Amazentis / Timeline — Urolithin A Patents

### Corporate Structure

- **[[Amazentis]] SA** (Lausanne): originator, IP holder, holds 50+ global patents
- **[[Timeline Nutrition]]**: consumer brand for Mitopure; marketing/distribution vehicle
- **January 2024:** L'Oréal and Nestlé Health Science invested CHF 56M (Series D) in Timeline

### European Patent Portfolio

| Patent | Title | Status |
|---|---|---|
| **EP3087065B1** | "Synthesis of urolithins" — manufacturing process | Active (granted) |
| **EP3369420B1** | "Non-therapeutic use of urolithin A for cognitive function" | Active (granted) |
| **EP3787615A1** | "Urolithin A as immune enhancer" | Application / examination |
| **EP3793984A1** | "Urolithin A and derivatives for use in therapy" | Application |
| **EP2862568A1** | "Compositions comprising urolithins — insulin secretion" | Older application |

### What Is Protected vs. Free

| Status | Content |
|---|---|
| **Protected** | Synthetic Urolithin A (≥97% purity), synthesis process, Mitopure formulation, therapeutic and non-therapeutic applications |
| **Not protected** | Natural Urolithin A formed in body from ellagitannins; **pomegranate/walnut extracts as UA precursors**; raw botanical extracts with naturally occurring low UA concentrations |
| **Grey zone** | Concentrated UA extracts from pomegranate marketed as "Urolithin A" — Freedom-to-Operate analysis required |

### Novel Food Authorization

- Amazentis obtained EU Novel Food authorization 2022 (application 2018-0538)
- **Proprietary authorization**: initially only Amazentis / Timeline may use it commercially
- Alternatives for third parties: (a) own Novel Food application (~2–5 years, €500k–2M), or (b) license from Amazentis
- Authorized forms: breakfast cereals, protein bars, supplements, medical foods, sports nutrition

### Patent Expiry

- Core synthesis patent EP3087065: filed ~2014–2016 → **expires ~2034–2036**
- Until then: synthetic Urolithin A cannot be legally produced in EU without license

### Licensing Model

- Timeline sells Mitopure raw material to B2B partners
- No public price list; typical industry models: 5–15% royalty on net revenue OR fixed price/kg with MOQ
- **B2B contact:** bd@timeline.as

### Recommendations for Performance Coffee Brand

| Option | Risk | Cost | Recommendation |
|---|---|---|---|
| **A: Pomegranate extract** (standardized ≥40% punicalagin/ellagic acid) | None | Low (~20–50 €/kg) | **✅ Recommended for MVP** |
| **B: License Mitopure from Timeline** | Low (if licensed) | Unknown (MOQ + royalty) | Possible but likely cost-prohibitive for early-stage |
| **C: Generic Urolithin A from China** | HIGH | Low | ❌ Not recommended — no EU Novel Food approval, possible patent infringement |

## 2. ChromaDex / Niagen Bioscience — NR (NIAGEN) Patents

### Corporate Structure (2025 Update)

- **ChromaDex Corp.** rebranded to **Niagen Bioscience, Inc.** (NASDAQ: NAGE) in 2025
- Core NR patents originated from Dartmouth College (Charles Brenner research); ChromaDex licensed exclusively

### Patent Portfolio

| Patent | Content | Expiry |
|---|---|---|
| US 8,197,807 | Composition patent on NR | ~2030 |
| US 12,252,506 (March 2025) | NR salt forms (NR Malate, NR Tartrate) | ~2034 |
| European patents | NR in multiple EU countries | Research required (espacenet.epo.org) |

### Licensing

- Niagen Bioscience licenses NIAGEN to brands; known licensees: Nestlé Health Science (global framework, $5M investment 2022), various supplement brands
- No public B2B pricing
- **Contact:** partnerops@niagenbio.com or chromadex.com/b2b

### Generic NR Assessment

- No validated alternative to NIAGEN with equivalent clinical evidence
- Chinese generic NR: EU patent status unclear, quality control questionable (87% of NR supplements fail label claims per ChromaDex study), regulatory risk
- **Recommendation:** License NIAGEN if NR is in the stack. "Patentiertes NIAGEN®" is a communication advantage at premium positioning.

### NR vs. NMN Comparison

| Feature | NR (NIAGEN) | NMN |
|---|---|---|
| EU legal status | Novel Food (approved via ChromaDex route?) | Novel Food — not approved, grey zone |
| Patent landscape | Clear (ChromaDex/Niagen Bioscience) | Complex, less clear |
| Bioavailability | Well evidenced | Comparable, fewer human studies |
| Price | Higher (license required) | Lower (generic available) |
| EU safety | Higher | Medium regulatory risk |

## 3. NMN — EU Status and Patent Landscape

### Regulatory Status EU (June 2026)

- NMN = Novel Food under EU 2015/2283
- **No approved NMN product in EU as of mid-2026**
- **May 2026:** EFSA issued positive Safety Opinion for NMN (applicant: EffePharm, China) — important milestone but not immediate authorization; European Commission must still issue implementing regulation
- Additional EFSA examination underway (Shanghai Shangke Biotechnology, initiated July 2025)

### Germany-Specific

- NMN supplements sold de facto in Germany (online, health stores) but formally non-compliant without Novel Food authorization
- No active prohibition, no explicit tolerance → **legal grey zone**

### NMN Patents

- **NMN substance itself is not patentable** (naturally occurring)
- **Metro International Biotech (David Sinclair, Harvard):** Patent on beta-NMN crystal forms (US11059847B2, filed 2019); pharmaceutical development MIB-626
- Chinese manufacturers hold synthesis process patents
- **Practical assessment:** NMN as supplement ingredient NOT patent-blocked in EU, but **NOT regulatorily approved**

### Recommendation for Performance Coffee Brand

| Timeline | Recommendation |
|---|---|
| **Short-term (Launch 2026)** | **Do NOT include NMN in MVP stack** — regulatory risk too high for new company without compliance resources |
| **Medium-term (2027+)** | Re-evaluate when positive EFSA Opinion translates to EU regulation |
| **Immediate alternative** | NR (NIAGEN) as NAD+ booster — better regulated, clinically comparable |

## 4. Additional Ingredients — IP & Regulatory Assessment

### Spermidine

- **EU Regulatory:** Spermidine-rich wheat germ extract approved under EU Implementing Regulation 2020/443; max 6mg spermidine/day; content 0.8–2.4 mg/g in extract
- **Patent:** EP3320899A1 — "Use of spermidine for mitochondrial respiration enhancement"; likely covers specific therapeutic application, probably not general supplement use
- **Recommendation:** ✅ Green light — purchase EU Novel Food-compliant ware (e.g., Longevity Labs GmbH, Graz); comply with 6mg/day limit

### Fisetin

- **EU Regulatory:** Natural flavonoid (strawberries, apples, onions); **Novel Food status unclear** for isolated extract/concentrate — depends on concentration and form
- **Patent:** No broad patent on fisetin as supplement; patents exist for derivatives (University of Minnesota, 2024)
- **Recommendation:** Clarify Novel Food status before use; high doses (>100mg) may be classified as Novel Food; likely too complex for MVP

### Ellagic Acid / Punicalagin Extract

- **Status:** Completely patent-free; no Novel Food issues (established botanical extract)
- **Suppliers:** Sabinsa Corp. (40% punicalagin), Nexira, Nutraceutix
- **Communication:** "Supports the body's natural mitophagy activation" — avoid "Urolithin A" claims to minimize IP risk

### Lion's Mane (Hericium erinaceus)

- **Status:** Naturally occurring mushroom; no broad patents on standardized extracts
- **Recommendation:** ✅ Use standardized extracts (min. 30% beta-glucans, from fruiting body — not mycelium); no IP barrier

### KSM-66 Ashwagandha

- **Protection:** Ixoreal Biomed Inc. holds trademark "KSM-66 ASHWAGANDHA" (US #4551163, active, renewed 2024) + process patent (green chemistry, alcohol-free, root-only) + WO2023141607A2
- **What's protected:** Name KSM-66, specific extraction process, formulations
- **What's free:** Ashwagandha plant; any Ashwagandha extract
- **Recommendation:** If KSM-66 label desired → buy from Ixoreal directly; alternatively Sensoril Ashwagandha (Natreon Inc.) or generic root extract (min. 2.5% withanolides)

### Sensoril Ashwagandha

- **Protection:** Natreon Inc. trademark on "Sensoril"; proprietary leaf+root extract (min. 8% withanolides); no known broad EU patents on process
- **Recommendation:** Purchase Natreon-sourced ware or use generic

### Caffeine / Chlorogenic Acid (from Coffee)

- **Status:** Both patent-free and established; EFSA guidelines apply (max 400mg caffeine/day for adults)
- **Recommendation:** ✅ No IP issues; light roast preferred for chlorogenic acid content; stay within EFSA limits

## 5. EUIPO Trademark Strategy

### Relevant Classes for Performance Coffee Brand

| Class | Content | Priority |
|---|---|---|
| **Class 30** | Coffee, tea, cocoa, coffee-based preparations, instant coffee | **MVP — mandatory** |
| **Class 5** | Food supplements, dietary preparations, vitamin preparations, supplement sticks | **MVP — mandatory** |
| **Class 32** | Non-alcoholic beverages, energy drinks (for RTD scaling) | Medium-term |
| **Class 35** | Retail, online commerce, e-commerce | Optional (strong DTC strategy) |

**MVP recommendation:** 3 classes: 30 + 5 + 32

### EUIPO Fees (2025/2026)

| Item | Amount |
|---|---|
| Online application, 1st class | 850 € |
| 2nd class | +50 € |
| 3rd class | +150 € |
| **Total 3 classes (authority fees)** | **1,050 €** |
| Lawyer (DE/EU, EUIPO filing incl. search) | 1,500–3,500 € |
| **Estimated total** | **2,500–4,500 €** |

> ⚠️ **Note:** Source document states "ca. 1.150 € (Behörde)" in the executive summary but the detailed table shows 1,050 €. The detailed table (850 + 50 + 150 = 1,050 €) is the authoritative figure.

### Timeline

- EUIPO Fast Track (same fee): ~5–7 months from application to registration if no opposition
- Without opposition: ~4 months examination + 3 months opposition period

### SME Fund 2026

- EU program refunds small companies up to 50% of EUIPO fees (up to ~1,000 €)
- Check annually: euipo.europa.eu/de/sme-fund

### Filing Strategy

**Rule:** File immediately after final naming decision, **before** first public appearance (website, social media, press releases).

**Pre-filing:** Research identical/similar marks in Classes 30/5/32 at EUIPO TMview (tmview.eu); collision search by lawyer is mandatory.

**Recommended lawyers:** Fachanwalt für gewerblichen Rechtsschutz with EUIPO experience; Berlin boutiques: Dr. Bongen Rembold Apel, Zirngibl (cost-efficient for startups); larger: Schalast, Freshfields.

## 6. GmbH Structure & IP Ownership

### When to Found GmbH

- **At latest:** Before first revenue or large-scale purchasing (liability protection)
- **Ideally:** Before EUIPO trademark application (GmbH as trademark owner from start)
- **Required:** Before investor discussions

**Costs:** GmbH min. €25,000 share capital (€12,500 immediately payable); notary ~€500–1,500; Handelsregister ~€150. Alternative: UG (haftungsbeschränkt) from €1.

### IP Holding Options

| Option | Description | Recommendation |
|---|---|---|
| **A: IP in operative GmbH** | Simplest; IP and operations in one entity | ✅ Recommended Phase 1 (<€500k revenue) |
| **B: Separate IP Holding GmbH** | IP-GmbH licenses to Operating GmbH; reduces taxable income via royalties; requires 2nd annual statement, transfer pricing documentation | Consider from €1–2M revenue or before investor rounds |

### IP Box / No German IP Box

- **Germany has NO IP Box regime** — deliberately not introduced
- **Lizenzschranke** (§4j EStG, since 2018): limits deductibility of license payments to companies in low-tax jurisdictions

### Foreign IP Box Options (for reference)

| Country | IP Box Rate | Conditions |
|---|---|---|
| Netherlands | 9% on IP income | Substance requirements, DEMPE activities |
| Ireland | 6.25% on qualifying income | R&D nexus required |
| Austria | 12.5% (patent box) | Patents and R&D activities |
| Luxembourg | 6.8% (net IP box) | Substance requirements |

**Reality check:** For a startup with <€2M revenue, a foreign IP holding structure is NOT economically sensible (setup €10,000–50,000 + ongoing €5,000–15,000/year). Also: German Lizenzschranke limits payments to low-tax countries.

**Recommendation Phase 1 (€0–2M):** All IP in German GmbH — simple, cheap, clean.

## 7. Recipe Protection: Trade Secret vs. Patent

### Patentability Assessment

Theoretically patentable for Performance Coffee Brand:
- Novel formulation combination (specific ingredient ratios with synergistic effect, scientifically evidenced)
- Manufacturing process (e.g., specialized encapsulation for oxidation-sensitive ingredients)
- Delivery form (e.g., unique carrier system for water-insoluble compounds in coffee stick)

**Realistic assessment:** Most functional coffee formulations are NOT patentable because:
- All ingredients are known
- Combinations without surprising synergistic effect are not patentable
- Prior art (existing competitive products) reveals very similar combinations

### Trade Secret Advantages

- Time-unlimited protection (as long as secrecy maintained)
- No disclosure obligation (recipe stays internal)
- No filing/examination costs
- Protects non-patentable innovations (ratios, sourcing strategies)

### Trade Secret Disadvantages

- No monopoly right — parallel independent development by competitor is legal
- Hard to protect against employee turnover / supplier changes
- No enforcement against independent development

### Trade Secret Legal Requirements (GeschGehG + EU Trade Secrets Directive)

1. **NDAs** with all suppliers, contract manufacturers, employees (before any disclosure)
2. **Access controls:** Documented access to recipe limited to need-to-know
3. **Documentation:** Internal timestamps for recipe development process
4. **Contractual clauses:** IP clauses in employment and supplier contracts

### Patent Cost Reference (EP Patent)

| Phase | Cost |
|---|---|
| Patent attorney filing (DE first application) | €3,000–8,000 |
| EP validation fees | €1,500–3,000 |
| Annual maintenance fees | €500–2,000/year |
| Examination fees, translations | €3,000–10,000 |
| **Total to EP grant** | **€20,000–60,000** |
| Defense costs (opposition/nullity action) | €50,000–200,000 |
| Timeline to EP grant | 3–6 years |

### Recommendation: Trade Secret as Primary Strategy

**Trade secret + trademark protection** is the recommended approach:
1. Patentable core invention unlikely for standard functional coffee formulations
2. €20,000–60,000 patent costs not sensible for early-stage startup
3. Main competitive advantage lies in **brand, community, sourcing quality** — not recipe
4. Trade secret provides practically sufficient recipe protection
5. Instead: invest budget in **EUIPO trademark + NIAGEN license + Kölner Liste certification**

**Exception:** If a genuine process innovation emerges (e.g., proprietary stability system for NR in aqueous solution), then point-in-time assessment by patent attorney.
