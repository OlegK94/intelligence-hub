# Peter Attia

## Overview

**Peter Attia, MD** is a physician specializing in the science of longevity and performance optimization. He is the host of **The Drive** podcast and author of *Outlive: The Science and Art of Longevity* (2023). He is one of three named reference authorities in [[Oleg Personal Context|Oleg]]'s performance system (alongside [[Andrew Huberman]] and [[Bryan Johnson]]).

## Key Frameworks

### ApoB as Primary Cardiovascular Risk Marker
- Attia argues that **ApoB** (Apolipoprotein B) is the most important single cardiovascular risk marker — superior to LDL cholesterol in predictive value
- ApoB measures the number of atherogenic lipoprotein particles (each LDL, VLDL, IDL, and Lp(a) particle carries one ApoB molecule)
- Must be **specifically requested** — not included in standard lipid panels
- Directly referenced in [[Performance Overview Source Detail]]: *"ApoB separat anfordern — nicht in Standardpanels enthalten"*
- Tracked in [[Blutbild Panel]]

### Zone 2 Training
- Attia is one of the strongest proponents of Zone 2 cardio for metabolic health and longevity
- Zone 2 is the intensity at which the body primarily uses fat oxidation for fuel — below the first lactate threshold
- Builds mitochondrial density, improves metabolic flexibility, and enhances aerobic capacity
- Directly aligns with [[Zone 2 Cardio]] as primary aerobic protocol and the primary limitator identified in [[Performance Overview]]

### Metabolic Health
- Insulin resistance, visceral fat, and metabolic syndrome as root causes of chronic disease
- WHR and visceral fat distribution as key risk indicators — consistent with Oleg's WHR 0.92 concern
- See [[3D Body Scan Scaneca Mai 2026]] for Oleg's metabolic risk markers

### Longevity Medicine Framework ("Medicine 3.0")
- Proactive, prevention-focused medicine vs. reactive disease treatment
- Early biomarker identification
- Exercise as the most powerful longevity intervention
- Strength, VO2max, and metabolic health as the "four horsemen" prevention levers

### The Drive Podcast
- Long-form episodes covering nutrition, exercise science, metabolic health, cancer prevention, cognitive health
- Frequently features researchers: Rhonda Patrick, David Sinclair, Layne Norton, and others
- Notable for depth and willingness to update positions based on evidence
