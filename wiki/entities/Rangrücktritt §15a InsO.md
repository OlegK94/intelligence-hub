# Rangrücktritt — §15a InsO

## Overview

A **Rangrücktritt** is a contractual declaration by [[Oleg Personal Context|Oleg]] as Gesellschafter (shareholder) of [[Wagglz GmbH]] that his **Gesellschafterdarlehen** (shareholder loan) is **nachrangig** (subordinated) to all other creditors. This is a **P0 priority** legal action with a **2026-06-15 deadline**.

> For full legal detail and source document analysis, see [[Rangrücktritt §15a InsO Source Detail]].

## Why This Is Critical

Without the Rangrücktritt:

| Risk | Detail |
|---|---|
| **§15a InsO Antragspflicht** | Oleg as GF is **immediately obligated** to file for insolvency |
| **Strafbarkeit** | Failure to file = **criminal offense** under §15a Abs. 4 InsO |
| **Zivilhaftung** | Civil liability to creditors of Wagglz GmbH |

## Legal Mechanism

### Problem
Wagglz GmbH has a balance sheet **Fehlbetrag (deficit) of 27,926.89 €**. This deficit triggers **§19 InsO Überschuldung** — the insolvency test for companies. The Gesellschafterdarlehen (Oleg's loan to the company) is currently counted as a liability, creating the deficit.

### Solution: Rangrücktritt
By declaring the Gesellschafterdarlehen subordinated to all other creditors:
1. The loan is **excluded from the §19 InsO liability calculation**
2. The balance sheet **Überschuldung is eliminated**
3. The **§15a InsO Antragspflicht lapses** — no obligation to file for insolvency
4. Oleg's personal **criminal and civil liability risk is removed**

### Legal Chain
```
Gesellschafterdarlehen → Rangrücktritt → Excluded from §19 Prüfung
→ Überschuldung eliminated → No §15a Antragspflicht → No criminal liability
```

## Rechtliche Grundlagen

- **§15a InsO:** Insolvenzantragspflicht (insolvency filing obligation); Abs. 4 = criminal sanction for GF who fails to file
- **§19 InsO:** Überschuldungsprüfung — the two-stage test: (1) balance sheet test, (2) going-concern prognosis
- **Rangrücktritt:** Standard German insolvency law instrument; recognized by courts and documented in extensive case law
